About

Genius Annotation

A Guns N' Roses masterpiece, usually the last song in every setlist, “Paradise City” was the sixth track for debut studio album Appetite for Destruction, and upon its single release in November 1988, reached #5 in the US and #6 in the UK. The lyrics follow a man frustrated with the big city life, combining a singalong “take me back home!” chorus with “this town is tearing me apart” verses. It’s also the only track in Appetite for Destruction featuring a synth.

The song appears as the theme song for the EA video game Burnout: Paradise, which is set in the fictional “Paradise City.”

Genius Answer

Slash has mentioned numerous times that “Paradise City” is his favorite Guns N' Roses song. It was written by him while playing random tunes on his guitar with the rest of the band on a tour bus. The original lyric concept was very different, as Slash recalls:

‘Take me down to the Paradise City where the girls are fat and they got big titties,’ I think that was my original lyric for it and the other guys changed it. We all thought it was funny but it wasn’t going to make it on the album. I think that’s how it went.
